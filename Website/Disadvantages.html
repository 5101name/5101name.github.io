CRISPR applications in cancer treatment

Disadvantages

- Long initial time investment: CRISPR requires a robust genome-editing pipeline, however not all labs have an established genome-editing pipeline. Without a robust genome-editing pipeline CRISPR will take a very long time to setup and optimize.

- The inefficiency of CRISPR's: Editing efficiency refers to the proportion of successfully editing of cells in your cultured vessel. CRISPR genome-editing efficiency can be influenced by many factors which can result in unexpected outcomes such as the unedited cells within a population.

- The unintended side-effects: Although the CRISPR-Cas9 technology is extremely specific in theory, it is not in actual use. Off-target alterations, also referred to as mutations, can occur elsewhere in the genome. Off-target effects are unpredictable and may adversely affect other genes or parts of the genome. This must be taken into consideration when discussing the findings.
